Scan Duration

The scan time is closely tied to the resolution and quality settings in laser scanning. Resolution dictates the precision of the scanner and the density of points that will be obtained. The default resolution of the scanner is 40,960 x 17,068. This means that the scanner can gather 699,105,280 points per scan and achieve an accuracy of 1.55mm at 10m This value scales linearly with distance, this means that the scanner can achieve 0.155mm at 1m to reduce both the time to scan and the number of data points collected we adjust the resolution to 1/2, 1/4, 1/8 depending on our requirements.

The resolution scaling occurs on both the X and Y axis. This means that 1/2 resolution = 1/4 the number of points or 20,480 x 8,534 = 174,776,320. The benefit of this approach means you can take 4x the amount of scans and achieve the same point density. This allows the operator to approach multiple angles and achieve more coverage. The downside of lowering resolution is the accuracy decreases, as there are less points taken in the same arc angle the accuracy will be worse. Half resolution is 3.1mm at 10m.

The quality setting dictates the number of times the scanner takes a laser reading. If the quality setting is set to 4x, the scanner will repeat the measurement 4 times. The scanner will then compare the results from the numerous datapoints and exclude the readings that are not accurate. Taking a greater number of measurements results in higher accuracy and confidence in data. The scanner using a 1,550nm laser, the laser will scatter easily when it reflects off a highly reflective surface. Taking repeated measurements means we lower the chance of collecting incorrect data.

Careful consideration must be taken when selecting the scanner settings. If the incorrect settings are chosen for the job the data recieved may not be of sufficent quality to satisfy our requirements. On the otherhand data recieved could exceed the quality required and time would be wasted on processing unnessessary data.

Connecting to the Scanner

The scanner operates on its own dedicated Wi-Fi network, necessitating a connection to facilitate data transfer between our device and the scanner for various essential tasks, including on-site registration. This connectivity enables seamless communication and ensures the successful execution of operations that require data exchange between our equipment and the scanner.

Connecting to Wi-Fi

The scanner's wireless network should be pre-configured by default. In the event of connectivity problems in subsequent sections, users are advised to consult the following section for resolution. Specifically, navigate to the "Faro Focus Premium V9 Settings" section of this manual.

The technical information will be found in "Manage," then "General," and finally "General Settings," where you will find the "WLAN" settings. It is important to note that these settings should remain untouched unless the user possesses advanced technical skills, as any alterations may lead to operational issues.

Connecting to the Scanner

Now that we are connected to the scanner's wireless local-area network (WLAN), we can connect to the user interface. The user interface (UI) is the same as that can be seen on the scanner its self, connecting to the UI gives the operator the ability to control all aspects of the scanner remotely with the additional benefit of a larger screen.

To connect to the WebGUI, we must navigate to our browser of choice.
